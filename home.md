# Wordaround

### Words With a New Spin

<img src="/images/Wordaround_Icon.png" width="150" alt="Wordaround App Icon">

Two letters are locked in place. The rest are up to you.

**Wordaround** is a free word puzzle game with a twist: every puzzle starts with two locked letters fixed in random positions. Your job is to build as many words as you can around them. No ads, no paywalls — just a thoughtfully crafted word game from a solo indie developer.

[![Download on the App Store](https://developer.apple.com/assets/elements/badges/download-on-the-app-store.svg)](https://apps.apple.com/app/id6759630399)

-----

## How It Works

Each puzzle gives you a set of letter tiles with two **locked letters** you can’t change. Type letters into the remaining tiles to form words — the game recognizes them automatically as you go.

It’s a simple constraint that creates a surprisingly deep challenge. Some configurations are wide open with dozens of possibilities. Others leave you staring at two awkward consonants wondering if any five-letter word has a K in the second slot and a Z in the fourth.

## Three Ways to Play

**Puzzle of the Day** — A new puzzle every day, the same for everyone worldwide. Difficulty builds through the week: Monday starts easy, Friday is the boss fight.

**Practice Mode** — Unlimited puzzles at any difficulty. Choose your word length, play at your own pace.

**Progressive Mode** — The locked letters stay put, but their positions shift each round. No timer — just escalating difficulty.

## Set Your Vibe

Each Vibe is a combination of a color theme and background music that changes the whole atmosphere. Play in Chill, Focus, or Energy — whatever fits your mood. Seasonal and special Vibes are coming in future updates.

-----

## Free. Really.

No ads. No gated features. No timers. Every mode, every difficulty, every Vibe is available from the first launch. The only in-app purchases are optional tip jars — they don’t unlock anything.

-----

## Built by One Person

Wordaround is made by Josh, a professional iOS engineer by day and indie game developer by night. Every line of code, every design decision, and every sound effect was made by a single developer who wanted complete creative control over something from concept to launch.
